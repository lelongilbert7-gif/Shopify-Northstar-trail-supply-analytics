# Shopify Data Analysis Report

## Overview
This report analyzes sales, customer behavior, product performance, and refund patterns for a Shopify-based coffee business.  

The objective is to identify key revenue drivers, operational risks, and opportunities for improving profitability.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Revenue Overview
- Gross Revenue: $286,446  
- Net Revenue: $271,218  
- Revenue Lost to Refunds: $15,228 (~5.3%)

Refunds account for a moderate share of revenue. While not excessive, they represent a meaningful cost that directly impacts profitability.

---

### 2. Product Performance
Revenue is highly concentrated among a small set of core products.

The top product, **Trail Brew Blend 12oz**, contributes approximately **20%** of total revenue, followed by **High Ridge Espresso 12oz** at **17%**.

Combined, the top 2 products generate over **36% of total revenue**, while the top 4 contribute more than half of all sales.

#### Business Implications:
- The business relies heavily on a limited number of high-performing SKUs  
- Supply chain stability for top products is critical  
- There is an opportunity to grow mid-tier products to reduce concentration risk  

---

### 3. Customer Behavior
A significant portion of revenue (~$48K) comes from orders without a customer ID, indicating guest checkouts or tracking gaps.

Among identified customers, the highest spender contributed approximately $1,183, with most top customers spending under $1,000.

This indicates a **high-volume, low individual spend model**, with revenue distributed across many customers rather than concentrated among a few high-value buyers.

#### Business Implications:
- Limited visibility into customer behavior reduces marketing effectiveness  
- The absence of identifiable high-value customers limits retention strategies  
- Improving customer tracking could unlock significant value  

---

### 4. Revenue Trends
Revenue shows a steady upward trend throughout 2024, growing from approximately $8K in January to over $20K in December.

Strong performance is observed in Q4, indicating clear seasonality, likely driven by holiday demand.

In early 2025, revenue declines from the peak but stabilizes at a higher baseline (~$15K–$16K), suggesting sustained growth.

#### Business Implications:
- Q4 is a critical revenue period and should be prioritized  
- The business demonstrates strong growth momentum  
- Maintaining post-peak stability indicates improving demand or retention  

---

### 5. Refund Impact
Refunds total approximately **$15,228**, representing **~5.3% of gross revenue**.

This falls within a normal range but remains a meaningful cost factor.

#### Business Implications:
- Refunds should be actively monitored and optimized  
- Even small reductions (1–2%) could significantly improve net revenue  
- Potential drivers include product expectations, quality, or fulfillment issues  

---

### 6. Product-Level Refund Patterns
After removing low-volume noise, refund rates across core products range between **~5% and 7%**.

Subscription products and certain accessories show slightly higher refund rates (~10%), while core coffee products remain more stable (~6%).

#### Business Implications:
- Refund behavior appears systemic rather than isolated  
- Subscription offerings may carry higher churn or dissatisfaction risk  
- Core products are stable but have room for improvement  

---

### 7. Problem Product Analysis
Among high-volume products, the **Northstar Camp Mug** exhibits the highest refund rate (~6.7%), indicating a potential product-specific issue.

However, most major products cluster within a narrow refund range (~5–6%), reinforcing the presence of a broader systemic factor.

#### Business Implications:
- No single product is critically defective  
- Refund issues likely stem from broader operational or customer experience factors  
- Targeted improvements in product communication, fulfillment, or expectations could reduce refunds across the board
