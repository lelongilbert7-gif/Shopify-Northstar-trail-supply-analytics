# Shopify Data Analysis Report

## Key Insights

### Revenue
- Total gross revenue = 286446.85
- Total net revenue = 271218.39
- Revenue lost to refunds = 15228.46
- Approximately 5.31% of total revenue is lost to refunds, indicating [low/moderate/high] refund impact on overall performance.
### Top Products
### Top Products Performance

Revenue is highly concentrated among a small set of core products.

The top product, **Trail Brew Blend 12oz**, contributes approximately **20%** of total revenue, followed by **High Ridge Espresso 12oz** at **17%**.

Combined, the top 2 products account for over **36% of total revenue**, while the top 4 products contribute more than half of total sales.

This indicates a strong dependency on a limited number of SKUs, particularly within the core coffee product line.

#### Business Implications:
- Revenue concentration increases operational risk if top products underperform
- Inventory and supply chain for top SKUs are critical to maintain
- Opportunity exists to scale mid-tier products to reduce dependency

### Customers
### Customer Value Analysis

A significant portion of revenue (~$48K) is associated with orders lacking a customer ID, indicating either guest checkouts or data tracking gaps. This represents a major visibility issue, as high-value customers cannot be properly identified or retained.

Among identified customers, the top spender (Ava Martinez) contributed approximately $1,183, with other top customers spending under $1,000.

This suggests that revenue is not concentrated among a small group of high-value customers, but rather distributed across a broad customer base.

#### Business Implications:
- The business operates on a high-volume, low individual spend model
- Lack of identifiable high-value customers limits opportunities for targeted retention strategies
- Improving customer tracking (e.g., reducing guest checkouts) could unlock significant marketing and retention value
### Trends
### Monthly Revenue Trend

Revenue shows a clear upward trajectory throughout 2024, growing from approximately $8K in January to a peak of over $20K in December.

The business experienced steady growth in the first half of the year, followed by a strong performance in Q4, with November and December representing the highest revenue months.

This pattern suggests strong seasonality, likely driven by increased consumer demand during the holiday period.

In early 2025, revenue declines from the December peak but stabilizes in the $15K–$16K range, indicating a higher baseline compared to early 2024.

#### Business Implications:
- The business demonstrates strong year-over-year growth potential
- Q4 is a critical revenue period and should be prioritized for marketing and inventory planning
- Post-holiday declines are expected, but maintaining a higher baseline suggests improved customer retention or demand

### Refunds
### Refund Impact Analysis

Total revenue lost to refunds amounts to approximately **$15,228**, representing roughly **5.3% of total gross revenue**.

This indicates a moderate level of refund activity. While not immediately concerning, it is significant enough to impact overall profitability.

#### Business Implications:
- Refunds are a non-trivial cost factor and should be monitored closely
- Potential drivers may include product quality issues, customer expectations mismatch, or fulfillment problems
- Reducing refund rates by even 1–2% could materially improve net revenue
### Product-Level Refund Analysis

Initial analysis showed extremely high refund rates for certain products; however, these were driven by low-revenue items and are not statistically meaningful.

After filtering for products with significant revenue, refund rates across core products range between **6%–12%**.

Subscription-based products and certain accessory items exhibit higher refund rates (~10%+), while core coffee products maintain more stable refund rates around ~6%.

#### Business Implications:
- Refund issues are not isolated to a single product, suggesting a systemic driver (e.g., fulfillment, expectations, or product experience)
- Subscription products may require closer monitoring due to elevated refund/churn signals
- Core products are stable but slightly above optimal refund thresholds, indicating room for improvement
### Problem Product Analysis

After filtering for statistically significant products (based on revenue and transaction volume), refund rates across major products range between approximately **5% and 7%**.

The **Northstar Camp Mug** exhibits the highest refund rate (~6.7%) among high-volume products, indicating a potential product-specific issue.

However, most core products—including espresso and roast variants—show relatively consistent refund rates around ~5–6%.

This consistency suggests that refund behavior is not driven by a single problematic product, but rather by broader systemic factors.

#### Business Implications:
- Refund patterns indicate a likely system-wide issue (e.g., customer expectations, fulfillment, or product experience)
- The Camp Mug may require targeted investigation due to its relatively higher refund rate
- Improving product descriptions, customer education, or fulfillment quality could reduce refund rates across the board
- Monitoring high-volume products remains critical, as small improvements can significantly impact overall profitability
